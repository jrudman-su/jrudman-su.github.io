Life On Mars

Initial Reaction:

Although the Martian year is twice as long as Earth’s visitors would still experience winter and summer seasons because of the planet’s tilted axis. Mars rotates at almost exactly the same frequency as our planet thus making its days just 40 minutes longer than our own. Explorers would be indulged some mind-blowing sights, with vast canyons, giant mountains and sand dunes much like a desert. The sunrise and sunset would be blue creating a completly new perseption of reality.

Setting Up Camp:

The cold, coupled with a lack of oxygen, very low pressures and solar radiation would confine astronauts to their spacecraft or enclosed vehicles. This would limit the aboumt of walks in spacesuits. The first priorities for long term visitors would therefore be building some kind of shelter and setting up a power source through solar or nucular.

Homesick:

If we get serious about establishing a colony on Mars we would have to consider that we will want it to be as similar to Earth as possible. Currently, The atmosphere on Mars is extreme enough that if you were to pour a water on the surface it would either freeze, boil away, or do both at the same time. Some want to fix this by adding more carben dioxide to jump start it into being similar to earths atmosphere. This would mean that liquid water could be sustained on the Martian surface, possibly allowing plants to be grown, which in turn would increase oxygen levels.
